ABOUT

Mark Zuckerberg's journey began in a Harvard dorm room, where he co-founded Facebook in 2004. From its humble beginnings, the social media platform he co-created has evolved into a global force, redefining how we connect, communicate, and share information.

Mark's visionary approach extends to emerging technologies like VR and AI, reshaping how we work, play, and collaborate within the metaverse.

Early Life and Education

The Birth of facebook

  • Facebook was founded by Mark Zuckerberg, along with his college roommates Andrew McCollum, Eduardo Saverin, Chris Hughes, and Dustin Moskovitz. It originated in Zuckerberg's dorm room at Harvard University in February 2004. The initial purpose was to create a platform that allowed Harvard students to connect and interact with each other online.
  • On February 4, 2004, Mark Zuckerberg launched the website "Thefacebook.com" for Harvard students. The site's success quickly led to its expansion to other Ivy League universities and later to universities across the United States and Canada. It became a hit among college students for its simplicity and exclusivity.
  • In 2005, the company dropped "The" from its name and became simply "Facebook." This change reflected the company's desire to expand beyond colleges and universities and connect people worldwide. The decision to open the platform to high school students and eventually the general public marked a significant shift in its mission and reach.
  • Facebook's origin marked the beginning of a social media revolution. It introduced features like the "Wall" and "News Feed," which changed how people shared information and interacted online. Facebook's rapid growth and global expansion made it one of the widely used social networking platforms, transforming the way people connect, communicate, and share content.

Projects

Mark Zuckerberg's diverse projects include Meta Quest's VR accessibility, AI for social impact, bridging the digital divide with Internet.org, and his groundbreaking metaverse vision, redefining how we connect and interact.

Above all, his visionary metaverse concept promises to reshape work, entertainment, and social interaction, marking a new frontier in technology and human connectivity.
